Adobo Manok

Adobong manok is a popular Filipino dish that consists of chicken marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns, then simmered until tender. The result is a savory and slightly tangy dish that is often served with rice. Adobong manok is typically made with chicken legs or thighs, but it can also be made with other cuts of chicken, such as breasts. It is a staple dish in the Philippines and is enjoyed for its flavor, tenderness, and simplicity.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 (3 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 2/3 cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

Procedures

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken pieces until golden brown, 2 to 3 minutes per side. Transfer chicken to a plate and set aside.
  2. Add onion and garlic to the skillet; cook until softened and brown, about 6 minutes. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar and season with garlic powder, black pepper, and bay leaf.
  3. Return chicken to pan, increase heat to high, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until chicken is tender and cooked through, 35 to 40 minutes.
